What Is NeuroGuard?

NeuroGuard is a gamified health monitoring platform that turns routine neurological assessments into engaging mini-games. Using your phone's built-in sensors — touchscreen, accelerometer, gyroscope, microphone, and camera — NeuroGuard captures clinically relevant biomarkers across six modalities: motor function, cognitive performance, cardiovascular health, voice and speech patterns, gait and balance, and oculomotor tracking.

Behind the games, a multi-LLM clinical consensus engine (ICDL) analyzes your data using multiple independent AI models simultaneously, dramatically reducing the risk of false positives. An adaptive immune intelligence system learns your personal baseline over time and flags meaningful deviations, while an entropic hallucination detection mesh ensures AI outputs remain grounded and reliable.

Clinical-Grade AI Architecture: NeuroGuard's ICDL engine queries multiple AI models using Latin Square assignment with context rotation. Correlated failure detection identifies when models produce unreliable outputs, and automated architectural quarantine isolates compromised model pathways. This means every health insight you receive has been validated across independent AI systems.

The Game Engines

Each game is designed to feel fun while quietly extracting precise biomarker data. You play for a few minutes; the system captures hundreds of data points per session.

Motor Assessment

Quick Tap

A whack-a-mole-style game with separate left and right hand rounds. Measures tap speed, accuracy, reaction time, and touch pressure. Bilateral comparison detects hemispheric asymmetries that may indicate early neurological changes.

Motor & Tremor Analysis

Trace Perfect

Draw Archimedes spirals, clock faces, and copy figures on screen. FFT tremor analysis at 3-12Hz detects rest tremor, action tremor, and intention tremor with clinical-grade precision. Measures drawing accuracy and pen pressure patterns.

Cognitive Assessment

Memory Match Plus

Classic card matching combined with N-back tasks, spatial memory challenges, and interference tasks. Tracks working memory capacity, processing speed, attention, and recall accuracy over time.

Oculomotor Tracking

Pupil Pilot

Uses your phone's front camera to measure pupillary light reflex, saccadic eye movements, smooth pursuit tracking, convergence and divergence. Also tests color discrimination and contrast sensitivity.

Voice & Speech Biomarkers

Voice Quest

Guided vocal exercises that extract jitter, shimmer, harmonics-to-noise ratio (HNR), fundamental frequency (F0), formant frequencies, and spectral tilt. Changes in these biomarkers can indicate neurological or respiratory conditions.

Balance & Gait

Balance Master

Uses accelerometer and gyroscope data to assess standing balance, step cadence, stride length, and gait symmetry. Detects subtle changes in postural stability that may precede clinical symptoms.

Cardiovascular

Rhythm Doctor

A rhythm-tapping game that simultaneously measures heart rate, heart rate variability (HRV), RMSSD, and rhythm accuracy. Combines fun gameplay with passive cardiac monitoring through the phone's sensors.

Visuospatial

Tetris Rotation

A spatial rotation game that tests visuospatial abilities, mental rotation speed, and spatial working memory. Difficulty adapts based on your performance to maintain engagement and clinical sensitivity.

Additional game engines include Step Quest (step counting and gait analysis), Psychomotor Rhythm (bilateral motor coordination), MindScape (advanced cognitive challenges), MedVault (medical records storage), Nutri Track (nutrition tracking), Physio Quest (physical rehabilitation), Family Tree (family health history), Lifestyle Capture (activity logging), and AR Modes (augmented reality assessments) — 19 game engines in total, with specialized modes for industrial safety, military/aerospace, and elite athletics.

App Screens & How You Interact

Games Hub

Your Assessment Playground

The Games Hub presents all available game engines in a two-column grid. Each game shows its name, the biomarker modality it targets, estimated play time, and your last session score.

  • Active Games: Games you have unlocked and calibrated appear at the top with full-color cards. Tap any game to jump straight into a session.
  • Coming Soon: Upcoming game engines appear as grayed-out cards with a "Coming Soon" label and brief description of what they will measure.
  • Daily Recommendation: The system suggests which games to play today based on which biomarkers haven't been tested recently, ensuring balanced coverage across all modalities.
  • Session History: Tap the clock icon on any game to see your past sessions, scores, and biomarker trends specific to that game engine.
Health Dashboard

Biomarker Trends & Risk Scoring

The Dashboard is where game data becomes health intelligence. It synthesizes data from all your game sessions into a unified view of your neurological health.

  • Six-Modality Overview: Color-coded cards for Motor, Cognitive, Cardiovascular, Voice/Speech, Gait/Balance, and Oculomotor health. Each shows a current status (Normal, Watch, Alert) and trend direction.
  • Composite Risk Score: An overall score that combines data from all modalities, weighted by clinical significance. This is your at-a-glance neurological health indicator.
  • Trend Charts: Tap any modality card to see detailed sparkline charts showing how each biomarker has changed over days, weeks, and months.
  • Bilateral Comparison: A dedicated view showing left-hand vs. right-hand performance across motor games. Any growing asymmetry is highlighted with an explanation of its potential significance.
  • AI Insights: The ICDL consensus engine generates plain-language summaries of what your data shows, with confidence indicators showing model agreement levels.
Game Session

Playing a Game (Quick Tap Example)

Each game follows a consistent pattern: brief instructions, calibration, gameplay, and results. Here is how Quick Tap works as an example.

  • Instructions: A brief animation shows what to do. For Quick Tap: tap the targets as fast as you can when they appear. You will play one round with your left hand, then one with your right.
  • Calibration: A short practice round establishes your baseline for this session. The difficulty adapts to keep you in the optimal challenge zone.
  • Gameplay: Targets appear at random positions. You tap them as quickly and accurately as possible. The game feels like fun; meanwhile sensors capture reaction time, tap accuracy, touch pressure, and inter-tap interval for every single touch.
  • Results: After both rounds, you see your score, accuracy percentage, and how you compare to your personal average. Behind the scenes, the data is processed through the ICDL engine and added to your biomarker history.
Achievements

Progress & Motivation

The Achievements screen gamifies the monitoring process itself, rewarding consistent engagement with badges and streaks.

  • Daily Streaks: Track consecutive days of playing at least one game. Longer streaks unlock achievement badges.
  • Modality Coverage: Badges for testing all six modalities within a week, ensuring balanced biomarker data collection.
  • Personal Records: Track your best scores across all games. See improvement trends that keep you motivated to play regularly.
